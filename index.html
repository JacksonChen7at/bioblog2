Silent Heroes: Why Plants Matter

Introduction

Plants are one of the most important parts of life on Earth. They support almost every ecosystem by producing oxygen, food, and stability for the environment. Even though they are often overlooked, life would not exist in its current form without them.

Importance of Plants

Plants produce oxygen through photosynthesis, which is essential for living organisms. They also form the base of most food chains, meaning all animals depend on them directly or indirectly. Plants help regulate climate by absorbing carbon dioxide and storing carbon.

Roots help hold soil together and reduce erosion.
Forests improve air quality by filtering pollutants naturally.

Gymnosperms

Gymnosperms are seed-producing plants that do not produce flowers or fruits. Their seeds are exposed, often found on cones. They are usually evergreen and adapted to harsh environments such as cold or dry regions.

Angiosperms

Angiosperms are flowering plants and the most diverse plant group on Earth. Their seeds are enclosed in fruits, which helps protect and spread them. They include trees, flowers, grasses, and most food crops humans rely on.
