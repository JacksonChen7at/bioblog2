Introduction

Plants are one of the most important parts of life on Earth. They support almost every ecosystem by producing oxygen, food, and stability for the environment. Even though they are often overlooked, life would not exist in its current form without them.

Importance of Plants

Plants produce oxygen through photosynthesis, which is essential for living organisms. They also form the base of most food chains, meaning all animals depend on them directly or indirectly. Plants help regulate climate by absorbing carbon dioxide and storing carbon.

Roots help hold soil together and reduce erosion.
Forests improve air quality by filtering pollutants naturally.

Gymnosperms (pick 2)

Gymnosperm 1: Pine Tree (Conifer)

Has needles instead of leaves
Produces cones instead of flowers
Very common and easy to explain
Good example: survives cold climates

Gymnosperm 2: Cycad

Looks like a palm tree but isn’t one
Very ancient (lived during dinosaur times)
Produces cones
Grows in tropical areas

Alternative: Ginkgo tree — fan-shaped leaves, very unique

Fun Fact: Gymnosperms are some of the oldest seed plants on Earth.

Angiosperms

Monocot: Grass (or corn)

Long narrow leaves
Parallel veins
Roots spread out (fibrous roots)
Very common in everyday life

Dicot: Sunflower

Wide leaves
Veins branch out
Has flowers (pollination example)
Strong visual plant example

Alternative: rose, bean plant, oak tree

Best Combo: Pine, Cycad, Grass/Corn, Sunflower

Fun Fact: Angiosperms make up most of Earth’s plant species.
