Plant Biology Blog: Gymnosperms and Angiosperms

Gymnosperms

Gymnosperms are seed-producing plants that do not produce flowers or fruits. Their seeds are exposed, usually found on cones. These plants are some of the oldest seed plants on Earth and include conifers such as pine, spruce, and fir trees. They are well adapted to cold and dry environments and can survive in harsh climates.

Reproduction in gymnosperms occurs when wind carries pollen from male cones to female cones. After fertilization, seeds develop slowly and remain unprotected. Gymnosperms are important ecologically because they provide habitats for wildlife and are widely used for wood and paper products.

Angiosperms

Angiosperms are flowering plants and represent the most diverse group in the plant kingdom. Unlike gymnosperms, they produce flowers and fruits that protect their seeds, making them highly successful across many environments including forests, deserts, and grasslands.

Angiosperms rely on wind, insects, animals, and water for pollination. After fertilization, the ovary develops into a fruit that helps spread seeds. This group includes trees, grasses, flowers, fruits, and vegetables that are essential to human life and ecosystems.
