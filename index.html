Introduction

Plants are essential living organisms that support nearly all life on Earth. They provide oxygen, food, shelter, and stability to ecosystems across the globe. Without plants, animals and humans would not survive because the food chain begins with them. Plants also help regulate the atmosphere by absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen, making Earth suitable for life.

Fun Fact: Plants were the first living organisms to colonize land over 400 million years ago.

Importance of Plants

Plants play a critical role in maintaining balance in nature. Through photosynthesis, they convert sunlight into energy while producing oxygen as a byproduct. This oxygen is necessary for almost all living organisms. Plants also form the base of food chains, meaning every animal either directly or indirectly depends on them. In addition, plants help reduce climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide and storing carbon in their tissues. They also prevent soil erosion, improve air quality, and provide habitats for countless species.

Fun Fact: One large tree can provide enough oxygen for 2–4 people every day.
Fun Fact: Forests act as natural air filters by removing pollutants from the atmosphere.

Gymnosperms

Gymnosperms are a group of seed-producing plants that do not produce flowers or fruits. Instead, their seeds are exposed and are usually found on cones. These plants are often evergreen and are well adapted to survive in harsh environments such as cold forests, mountains, and dry regions. Gymnosperms are some of the oldest plant groups on Earth and have existed for millions of years.

Fun Fact: Some gymnosperms can live for over 1,000 years.

Angiosperms

Angiosperms are flowering plants and represent the most diverse group of plants in the world. Unlike gymnosperms, their seeds are enclosed inside fruits, which helps protect them and allows for easier dispersal by animals, wind, or water. Angiosperms include everything from trees and flowers to grasses and important food crops.

Fun Fact: Angiosperms make up about 80% of all known green plants on Earth.
