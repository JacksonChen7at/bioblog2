Plant Reproduction Overview

Plants reproduce in two major groups: gymnosperms and angiosperms. Gymnosperms produce naked seeds, usually found in cones, while angiosperms produce flowers and fruits. These adaptations help plants survive in different environments and spread their seeds efficiently.

Angiosperms are the most diverse group of plants on Earth. They use animals, wind, and water to spread pollen and seeds. Gymnosperms, on the other hand, mostly rely on wind for pollination.

Fun Fact

Some pine trees (gymnosperms) can live for over 4,000 years, making them some of the oldest living organisms on Earth.

Example Plant

The sunflower is an angiosperm. It tracks the sun during the day and produces thousands of seeds inside its flower head.
